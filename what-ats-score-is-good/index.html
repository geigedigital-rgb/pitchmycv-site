What ATS Score Is Good?

There is no universal "perfect" score, but practical score ranges can help you decide when your resume is ready for applications.

Practical ATS score ranges

  • Below 60: high risk of poor parsing or weak relevance.
  • 60-74: workable baseline, but likely missing role-specific signals.
  • 75-85: generally strong for most competitive applications.
  • 85+: excellent technical alignment, if readability remains natural.

Why score alone is not enough

A high ATS score does not automatically mean interview success. Recruiters still review clarity, credibility, and impact. If your resume sounds generic or lacks measurable outcomes, strong ATS performance may not convert into callbacks.

How to improve low ATS scores fast

  1. Fix formatting and section structure first.
  2. Add missing role keywords in context.
  3. Rewrite top bullets with numbers and business outcomes.
  4. Validate role fit in the resume job match checker.
