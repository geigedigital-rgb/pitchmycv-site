ATS Resume Checklist

Use this quick checklist before every job application to avoid parser issues and improve relevance against role requirements.

Quick answer: An ATS-ready resume should be text-readable, role-keyword aligned, and focused on measurable outcomes in each experience bullet.

1) Structure and format checks

  • Use a single-column layout whenever possible.
  • Avoid tables, charts, icons-as-text, and floating text boxes.
  • Use standard section headings: Summary, Experience, Skills, Education.
  • Submit in PDF or DOCX only when requested.

2) Keyword relevance checks

  • Match the exact role title and domain terms where accurate.
  • Include required hard skills and tooling mentioned in the job description.
  • Mirror variant terms only when valid (for example, "A/B testing" and "experimentation").
  • Place priority keywords in Summary, Skills, and recent Experience entries.

3) Achievement quality checks

  • Start bullets with strong action verbs.
  • Add quantifiable outcomes where possible (%, $, time saved, growth).
  • Show business impact, not only task ownership.
  • Keep bullets concise and scannable for recruiters.

4) Final pre-apply checks
