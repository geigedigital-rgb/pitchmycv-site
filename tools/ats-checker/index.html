What ATS resume checking covers on PitchCV

Applicant tracking systems rank and filter candidates before recruiters open files. This page explains the parsing and structure layer—whether machines can read dates, employers, and bullets reliably. Vocabulary and verbs matter next; they have dedicated tools so each URL stays focused for search engines and readers.

Machine-readable layout

Single-column flow, plain headings, and text-based skills blocks reduce the risk that Workday, Greenhouse, or Lever drop half of your experience during ingestion.

Tables, text boxes, and graphics

Creative PDFs often hide job titles or dates inside shapes. ATS checkers surface where extraction breaks so you can rebuild those sections in parser-friendly HTML or Word styles.

Section labels recruiters search

Consistent labels such as Experience, Education, and Skills help both ATS filters and human reviewers jump to proof points faster—especially for compliance and volume hiring queues.

Signals that commonly break ATS parsing

If your resume looks perfect on screen but ranks poorly, the issue is often invisible: the parser never joined your role title with its achievements. Use this checklist before you import.

  • Headers typed as text instead of Word styles—or custom text boxes for entire employment blocks.
  • Multi-column layouts where the second column is read before the first column finishes.
  • Icons or badges replacing plain-text labels for programming languages or certifications.
  • Footers with contact details only, while the body omits phone or city where the ATS expects it.
  • Merged table cells that collapse months or employer names when exported to plain text.
