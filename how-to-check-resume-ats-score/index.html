How to Check Resume ATS Score

An ATS score is useful only when you interpret it correctly. Treat it as a diagnostic system, not as a final hiring prediction.

Step 1: Run a clean baseline scan

Upload your current resume as-is in the ATS resume checker. Do not edit first. This gives you a true baseline and reveals which sections create parsing or relevance issues.

Step 2: Break score into components

Most low scores come from three sources: formatting errors, weak keyword coverage, and low evidence quality in bullets. Separate these factors before making changes so you do not waste time on cosmetic edits.

Step 3: Prioritize high-impact fixes

  • Fix structure first (section headers, dates, layout).
  • Add missing role keywords in context.
  • Strengthen top bullets with numbers and outcomes.

Step 4: Re-check with a target vacancy

Run the updated version against a real job post and compare with your baseline. Then test role alignment in the resume match tool for vacancy-specific accuracy.

Common ATS score mistakes

Trying to increase score by repeating keywords usually hurts readability. Instead, improve relevance with truthful role language and evidence-backed achievements.
